About this project

This dashboard communicates how extreme temperature and precipitation patterns influence soil moisture conditions over India. It combines ERA5 reanalysis data and IMD observational data to show spatial patterns, seasonal cycle, JJAS monsoon behaviour, trends, correlations and extreme events. The final result section supports drought-related interpretation by linking rainfall deficit, temperature stress and soil-moisture response in a simple decision-support format.

Study Area and Datasets

Study Area Name: India

India is selected as the study region to analyse climate extremes, monsoon rainfall behaviour and soil-moisture response. All spatial outputs are clipped/masked to the India region for dashboard interpretation.

Latitude Range6°N to 37.5°N

Dataset Description

Dataset 1: ERA5 Monthly Averaged Data

  • Period: 1990-2023 / selected dashboard range
  • Resolution: 0.25° × 0.25°
  • Source: ECMWF ERA5 reanalysis
  • Temperature: 2 m temperature, converted from Kelvin (K) to °C
  • Precipitation: Total precipitation, converted from metre (m) to millimetre (mm)
  • Soil Moisture: Volumetric soil water layer 1 (swvl1), unit m³/m³

Dataset 2: IMD Observational Data

  • Period: 1991-2023 / selected dashboard range
  • Temperature: Tmax and Tmin observational data, 1° × 1° resolution
  • Rainfall: Daily rainfall data, 0.25° × 0.25° resolution
  • Source: IMD Data Supply Portal (IMD DSP)
  • Temperature Unit: °C; mean temperature derived as (Tmax + Tmin) / 2
  • Rainfall Unit: mm; aggregated to monthly, seasonal and annual totals
